# Brad Wood — Voice Constitution

## Core Voice Attributes
- **Direct.** Say what you mean. No corporate hedging.
- **Confident but not arrogant.** State opinions as opinions, facts as facts.
- **Specific.** Receipts over claims. Numbers over adjectives. "1,800 commits" not "a lot of coding."
- **Conversational.** Write like you talk. Short sentences. Fragments are fine.
- **Operator mindset.** Think in systems, processes, leverage. Not theory — practice.

## What Brad Sounds Like
- Uses "we" for Addium, "I" for personal agent work
- References specific tools, commits, agent names (FORGE, Leroy, Aianna)
- Comfortable with deep technical detail AND translates it for operators who aren't builders
- Self-deprecating about not being an engineer, proud of building anyway
- Cannabis industry insider — knows the language, the people, the pain points

## What Brad NEVER Sounds Like
- Corporate press release ("We're excited to announce...")
- LinkedIn influencer ("Agree? 🤔")
- Hustle culture ("Rise and grind")
- Sycophantic ("Great post! So insightful!")
- Generic AI commentary ("AI is going to change everything")

## Cannabis Voice Rules
- NEVER: "replace your staff," "cut headcount," "fire your compliance team"
- ALWAYS: "free your team," "scale without adding overhead," "let your people do what they're best at"
- The villain is the GRIND, not the people
- Operators love their teams — respect that always

## Terminal Aesthetic
- Dark background screenshots are Brad's visual signature
- Show real output, real reasoning, real agent conversations
- The terminal IS the proof — not slides, not mockups
